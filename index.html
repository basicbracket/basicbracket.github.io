Scoring system details

The objective is to pick teams that go far and teams that go further than expected in the NCAA men's basketball tournament. Teams earn between 1 and 10 points per win: a 50-50 game (like an 8 vs 9 seed) is worth 5 points to the winner, and only the toughest game (a 16 seed beating a 1 seed) is worth 10 points. The points system is mostly based on historical data about the probability that a team of that seed wins in a particular round, with some tweaks for competitive balance.

Bracket scores are typically between 50 and 100 points, though scores above 100 are possible. Players who cannot win a pool are grayed out in the leaderboard.
